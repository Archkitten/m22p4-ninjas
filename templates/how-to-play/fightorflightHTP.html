Fight or Flight

Instructions:

Gameplay

There are two ways to win: Fight or Flight.
Each player has 3 health. The person that makes their opponent's health drop to 0 wins the Fight.
Each player has an escape meter. If your escape meter reaches max, you win by Flight.

Cards


Inspiration

A long time ago, back in elementary school, I played a game called double-paper-scissors. It was, of course, based on the popular game rock-paper-scissors, but with a slight twist; both players would turn around, choose one of the three options in the left hand, and one of the two remaining options in the right hand. At the same time, both players would turn around and the results would be calculated.

This mode had one advantage over rock-paper-scissors: ties were almost non-existent, unless both players happened to pick the same exact choices in the same exact order. The one flaw was still there though, that the game was entirely luck based. It was still extremely fun, and I hope to see a revival of the game someday!

Another thing in elementary and middle school was the popular game “double o seven”. This game involved 3 choices, similar to rock-paper-scissors, but with more depth. There was a gun option, a reload option, and a block option. You could block whenever, but using the gun required reloading the turn before. Rounds were continuous, and it was fast-paced fun. There was one problem though: you could theoretically block forever, causing both you and the other player to die of boredom.

I wanted to create a game similar to these fun playground psychology games that didn’t have the same issues: the ability to stall forever and lacking an element of skill. To counteract the first issue, I designed a game based around having two ways to win. This way, if players try to prevent their opponent from winning in one way, their opponent could just switch to a different way to win. With these two win conditions, I could now address the lack of skill in rock-paper-scissors; adding many different cards that counter one another, as well as the ability to tell which cards your opponent holds based on your current cards. Knowing how they play and which cards they have can give experienced players an advantage over newer ones.

I hope you all enjoy the end result! Have fun!
